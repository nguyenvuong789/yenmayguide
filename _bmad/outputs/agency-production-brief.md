# Yen May Vintage Agency Production Brief

## Status

Artifact: `[AB] Agency Production Brief`

Purpose:

Give the agency a practical production brief for the first shoot and first wave of content. The goal is to avoid generic fashion-shop content and launch Yen May with a distinct curation-led identity.

Primary channels:

- TikTok
- Instagram Reels/Stories/Feed
- Facebook Reels/posts
- Threads support posts
- Google Maps photo updates

## One-Page Agency Summary

If the agency only reads one section, read this.

Objective:

- Launch Yen May Vintage with 5 strong short videos that prove curation, make the hidden shop easy to find, and create DM/visit intent.

Main message:

- `Curated Y2K & vintage in Da Nang.`
- `Yên Mây không tuyển đồ theo số lượng. Mỗi món phải qua nhiều lớp chọn: nhãn mác rõ, chất vải tốt, form đẹp, tình trạng chỉn chu, màu sắc có gu và một điểm riêng đủ đáng giữ lại.`

First 5 videos:

1. POV Alley Reveal.
2. Reject Pile Number 1.
3. The 1 In 300 Hero Product.
4. Find Your Era Number 1.
5. Morning ASMR.

Non-negotiables:

- Owner stays private: no face-led founder content, no public owner name.
- No fake origin stories.
- No unverified authenticity claims.
- No TikTok watermark on Instagram/Facebook exports.
- Every product post needs confirmed availability before posting.
- Every video needs one clear CTA only.

Priority if time runs short:

1. TikTok/Instagram Reels edits for V1-V5.
2. Clean no-watermark exports.
3. Captions and cover frames.
4. Facebook native versions.
5. Google Maps photos.
6. Threads support notes.

The agency must deliver:

- 5 edited vertical videos in `9:16`.
- 5 clean no-watermark exports suitable for TikTok, Instagram, and Facebook.

- 5 cover frame options or thumbnail timestamps.

## Approval Workflow

No asset goes live without shop approval.

Approval owner:

- Shop/owner approves product truth: availability, size, price, condition, flaw, and curation reason.
- Agency approves technical export quality.
- Final publishing approval belongs to the shop unless the shop explicitly delegates posting.

Approval steps:

1. Agency sends first cuts and captions.
2. Shop checks product information and owner privacy.
3. Agency revises.
4. Shop gives final approval.
5. Only then publish or schedule.

Timing:

- First cuts should be delivered within the agreed post-production window.
- Shop should review with enough time to avoid rushed posting.
- If a product sells before publishing, update the caption or replace the product.

Do not boost any post without separate shop approval.

## Non-Negotiable Strategy

Yen May is not a generic thrift shop.

Yen May is:

- Curated Y2K & vintage in Da Nang.
- A hidden shop in An Hai Dong 1.
- A shop where every piece passes clear curation filters first.
- Quiet, warm, tactile, cinematic, and specific.

Core line:

`Yen May does not buy by volume. Every piece has to pass several filters: clear label, good fabric, strong shape, polished condition, tasteful color, and one detail worth keeping.`

Vietnamese:

`Yên Mây không tuyển đồ theo số lượng. Mỗi món phải qua nhiều lớp chọn: nhãn mác rõ, chất vải tốt, form đẹp, tình trạng chỉn chu, màu sắc có gu và một điểm riêng đủ đáng giữ lại.`

## First Shoot Goal

Create the first 5 strongest short videos for launch.

These videos must:

- Establish the hidden-shop identity.
- Prove curation, not just show clothes.
- Target `Y2K Da Nang` and `vintage shop Da Nang`.
- Work across TikTok, Instagram Reels, and Facebook Reels.
- Avoid showing the owner personally as a public personality.

## Tone Reference

The feel should be closer to:

- artisan workshop
- specialty coffee shop
- quiet design studio
- slow vintage archive

Not:

- loud fashion boutique
- generic thrift haul page
- fast-fashion shop
- discount shop
- over-edited TikTok trend page

## Do Not Do

- Do not open with logo/name zooms.
- Do not use generic upbeat boutique music.
- Do not use TikTok watermark links on Facebook.
- Do not make every video a product sale.
- Do not invent product origin stories.
- Do not show the owner as a public-facing character.
- Do not say exact era/year unless there is evidence.
- Do not overuse urgency/scarcity.

## Must Do In Every Video

1. Hook in the first `1.5s`.
2. Show proof: fabric, label, cut, reject reason, alley, shop detail, or product close-up.
3. Use relevant keyword in text overlay:
   - `Y2K Da Nang`
   - `vintage shop Da Nang`
   - `curated vintage Da Nang`
4. End with one action:
   - `Get Directions`
   - `DM to check size and availability`
   - `DM để giữ hàng`
   - `Save this for your Da Nang trip`
5. Keep owner-invisible:
   - hands only
   - POV
   - silhouette/back-shot only if needed and approved
   - no face-led founder content

## Language Rules

Use one primary language per asset. Do not mix English and Vietnamese randomly in the same overlay sequence.

Recommended language by video:

- V1 Alley Reveal: English-first. Vietnamese support optional in caption.
- V2 Reject Pile: Vietnamese-first for local trust, or create a separate English overlay version.
- V3 Hero Product: match the product target. Use Vietnamese for FB/local product sale, English for IG/TikTok foreign-facing product sale.
- V4 Find Your Era: English-first because it is educational and saveable.
- V5 Morning ASMR: English-first.

If creating bilingual captions:

- Put the primary audience language first.
- Keep the second language short.
- Do not translate every line if it makes the caption heavy.

## CTA Hierarchy

Use one primary CTA per asset.

- Discovery/location video: `Get Directions`
- Vietnamese product sale: `DM để giữ hàng`
- Foreign product availability: `DM to check size and availability`
- Instagram product Story: `DM to hold for 24h`
- Educational content: `Save this before your next vintage hunt`
- Travel-planning content: `Save this for your Da Nang trip`

Do not ask for DM, comments, saves, and directions with equal weight in the same post.

## Instagram Buying Flow For Product Content

Every Instagram product Story, Reel, Carousel, or Highlight should make buying easy.

Flow:

1. Customer sees item.
2. Customer DMs for size, price, availability, or more photos.
3. Shop replies with size, price, condition, curation reason, and more photos/video if needed.
4. If customer confirms, shop can hold for `24h`.
5. Customer chooses pickup or shipping.
6. Payment method: Vietnamese bank transfer.
7. After payment or final confirmation, update Story/Highlight with `sold` where useful.

Template:

English:

`Yes, this piece is available. Size: [x]. Price: [x]. Condition: [x]. We can hold it for 24h. Pickup at the shop or shipping is available. Payment by Vietnamese bank transfer.`

Vietnamese:

`Món này còn nha. Size: [x]. Giá: [x]. Tình trạng: [x]. Yên Mây có thể giữ hàng 24h. Bạn có thể ghé shop lấy hoặc hỏi ship. Thanh toán bằng chuyển khoản ngân hàng Việt Nam.`

## Urgency And Scarcity Rules

Use urgency only when true.

- Use `còn 1 chiếc` only when there is one piece.
- Use `sold` only after confirmed sale/payment.
- Do not write "đi nhanh" on every product.
- Use urgency mostly on product Stories/posts.
- Brand, discovery, and education posts should lead with proof, not pressure.

## Conversion And Retention Rules

Every asset should make the next step obvious.

- Product content: show size, price, condition, availability, curation reason, and DM prompt.
- Travel/discovery content: show address, hours, and `Get Directions`.
- Curation content: show one visible proof point before asking for action.
- Urgency claims must be true.

After purchase:

- Ask buyer to tag Yen May if they share an outfit photo.
- With buyer consent, repost customer fit photos or Stories as social proof.
- Repeat buyers can be added to Close Friends for early drop previews.
- Sold items can be marked `sold` in Stories/Highlights after sale/payment is confirmed.

Length:

Format:

Shot list:

3. Show one specific reason per piece:
   - fabric too thin
   - color faded badly
   - form not strong
   - heavy flaw
   - too generic
4. Put rejected pieces aside.
5. Show 2-3 pieces that passed.
6. Close-up of best passed detail.

Text overlay:

Vietnamese version:

- `Yên Mây không tuyển đồ theo số lượng`
- `Loại: vải quá mỏng`
- `Loại: form không giữ dáng`
- `Giữ lại: màu + form + tình trạng`

English version:

- `Yen May does not buy by volume`
- `Passed on: fabric too thin`
- `Passed on: weak shape`
- `Kept: color + shape + condition`

Tone rule:

- Do not ridicule rejected pieces.
- Say "not right for Yen May" or explain the reason neutrally.
- The tone should be educational, not superior.

Caption draft:

`Yên Mây không tuyển đồ theo số lượng. Mỗi món phải qua nhiều lớp chọn: nhãn mác rõ, chất vải tốt, form đẹp, tình trạng chỉn chu, màu sắc có gu và một điểm riêng đủ đáng giữ lại. DM để hỏi những món đã qua vòng chọn.`

- item type
- size

- condition
- curation reason

- `300k-500k`: add fabric, form, styling value.

## 4-Hour Shoot Structure

### 0:00-0:15 - Alignment

- Review shot list.
- Confirm 5 video order.
- Confirm owner-invisible rule.
- Confirm selected products and rejected products.
- Confirm who approves product info and final cuts.
- Confirm where files will be delivered.

### 0:15-1:00 - V1 Alley Reveal

- Capture vertical video.
- Capture 10-15 Google Maps photo candidates:
  - alley entrance
  - walking path
  - shop exterior
  - nearby visual cues

### 1:00-1:45 - V2 Reject Pile

- Shoot hands-only rejection sequence.
- Capture clean close-ups of rejects and passed pieces.
- Record natural fabric/rack audio.

### 1:45-2:30 - V3 Hero Product

- Shoot one hero product deeply.
- Capture label, fabric, cut, condition, flaw if any.
- Shoot Story card photos.

### 2:30-3:10 - V4 Find Your Era

- Shoot two comparison pieces.
- Capture clear detail shots.
- Keep text overlay simple.

### 3:10-3:50 - V5 Morning ASMR / Shop Atmosphere

- Shoot slow atmosphere clips.
- Capture Stories and Google Maps photos.

### 3:50-4:00 - Backup

- Reshoot weak angles.
- Capture extra product close-ups.
- Confirm files are backed up.
- Capture clean room tone and backup ASMR audio if possible.

Weather/noise contingency:

- If the alley is rainy, too noisy, or visually messy, prioritize interior/shop atmosphere and capture alley still photos later.
- If ambient audio is unusable, use clean separate fabric/rack/tag audio instead.
- Do not force V1 if the location looks bad that day; reschedule the alley reveal if needed.

## Curation Criteria For Agency

When filming products, always ask: why did this piece pass?

Valid reasons:

- clear brand/label/product signal
- reputable brand
- good fabric
- strong cut/form
- good condition
- minor flaw only, disclosed clearly
- unique color
- unique detail
- styling potential
- does not feel generic or disposable

Reject reasons to show:

- generic China/Guangzhou stock feel
- thin/disposable
- heavy stain/tear/deformation
- broken zipper/button
- major pilling/fabric damage
- forgettable even if wearable
- off-brand color or texture
