## Original Findings And Resolutions

The findings below are kept as an audit trail. They have already been patched into `_bmad/outputs/agency-production-brief.md`; see the resolution summary above for where each fix landed.

### P1 - IG buying loop was not fully closed

The brand foundation has an Instagram Buying Flow, but the agency brief does not include it. This creates friction because agency product Stories may miss hold/payment/shipping details.

Resolution applied:

Add IG DM purchase rules:

- Hold: 24h.
- Payment: Vietnamese bank transfer.
- Fulfillment: pickup or shipping.
- Product Story must include size, price, condition, availability, and DM prompt.

Psychology:

- Reduces Activation Energy.
- Reduces Regret Aversion.
- Creates Commitment without fake pressure.

### P1 - Urgency needed stricter governance

The brief says not to overuse urgency/scarcity, but product execution needs stricter rules.

Resolution applied:

Add:

- Use `còn 1 chiếc` only when true.
- Use `sold` only after confirmed sale/payment.
- Do not write "đi nhanh" on every product.
- Use urgency mainly on product Stories/posts, not brand/discovery content.

Psychology:

- Keeps Scarcity credible.
- Prevents trust decay.

### P1 - V3 needed stronger price-tier handling

Hero Product can be impulse, considered, or premium. The proof required differs by tier.

Resolution applied:

Add price-tier instructions:

- 150k-250k: short copy, price/size/condition, quick DM.
- 300k-500k: add fabric, form, styling value.
- 1M+: add label close-up, condition proof, retail/reference anchor only if accurate.

Psychology:

- Uses Mental Accounting and Anchoring correctly.

### P2 - V1 relies on hidden location but does not address "is it worth the trip?"

Foreign travelers need confidence before visiting. A hidden alley is exciting but also risky.

Patch recommendation:

Add one proof frame or caption line:

- `Real shop. Open daily 10:00-21:00.`
- `Curated Y2K & vintage, not a market stall.`
- `Save this before your Da Nang shopping day.`

Psychology:

- Reduces Loss Aversion.
- Makes the trip feel safer.

### P2 - V2 Reject Pile could trigger negative attribution if too harsh

If rejected pieces are shown too aggressively, viewers may feel the brand is judgmental.

Patch recommendation:

Use educational framing:

- "Why we pass on this" instead of "bad item".
- "Not right for Yen May" instead of "trash".

Psychology:

- Preserves Liking.
- Keeps Authority Bias from turning into arrogance.

### P2 - No retention prompt after purchase

The brief captures first conversion but not repeat behavior.

Patch recommendation:

Add a post-purchase content action:

- After purchase, invite buyer to tag Yen May.
- Repeat buyers can be added to Close Friends for early drops.
- Sold items can become social proof if customer consents.

Psychology:

- Commitment and Consistency.
- Endowment Effect.
- Mimetic Desire.

### P3 - Success signals lack conversion-rate thinking

The brief tracks views and DMs, but not whether those DMs convert.

Patch recommendation:

Add:

- DM inquiries from each video.
- Holds created.
- Holds paid.
- Store visits mentioning TikTok/IG/FB.
- Direction clicks from Google Maps.

## Highest-Leverage Psychological Patch

Add a `Conversion Rules` section:

- Every product post must make the next step obvious.
- Every travel/discovery post must reduce visit uncertainty.
- Every curation post must include one visible proof point.
- Every urgency claim must be true.

## Verdict

The original brief was psychologically strong for awareness and trust, but needed tighter conversion mechanics. The patched brief now includes the Instagram buying flow, urgency rules, price-tier logic, curation tone guardrails, retention prompts, and conversion tracking. It is ready to support the next workflow step unless the shop changes payment, hold, or shipping rules.
