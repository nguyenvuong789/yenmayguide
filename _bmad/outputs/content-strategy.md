## Strategic Objective

Build Yen May Vintage as Da Nang's most curated Y2K & vintage shop by making curation visible, making the shop easy to find, and turning product interest into either DM inquiries or physical visits.

Primary content goals:

1. Win Phase 1 search visibility for `Y2K Da Nang`.
2. Build trust in Yen May's selection standard.
3. Convert Vietnamese buyers through DM.
4. Convert foreign travelers through Instagram DM and shop visits.
5. Keep Google Maps fresh enough to support local discovery.

## Content Positioning

Core message:

`Curated Y2K & vintage in Da Nang.`

Plain-language message:

`Hand-picked Y2K and vintage pieces for people who want to dress like themselves, not everyone else.`

Vietnamese message:

`Y2K & vintage được chọn kỹ tại Đà Nẵng, dành cho người có gu riêng.`

Content should prove:

- Yen May selects, not dumps.
- The shop is worth finding.
- Products are explained, not just shown.
- Buyers can act easily: DM, reserve, or get directions.

## Copywriting Strategy

Every content asset should answer one clear question:

`Why should this person care now?`

Use this copy sequence:

1. Hook: stop the scroll with a specific claim, contrast, or question.
2. Proof: show the fabric, cut, reject reason, location, review, or product detail.
3. Meaning: explain why it matters to the buyer.
4. Action: give one next step.

Avoid clever copy that hides the action. Yen May can be poetic, but buying and visiting instructions must stay plain.

### Core Message Formulas

For Vietnamese product posts:

`[Item] được chọn vì [specific reason]. Size [x], tình trạng [x]. DM để giữ hàng.`

For foreign-facing product posts:

`Picked for its [fabric/cut/detail]. Size [x]. DM to check availability or visit us daily 10:00-21:00.`

For location/discovery posts:

`A hidden Y2K & vintage shop in An Hai Dong 1, Da Nang. Open daily 10:00-21:00. Get Directions.`

For curation proof:

`I looked through [number] pieces. This one made the cut because [specific reason].`

For education:

`Most people miss [detail]. Here is how to spot it before you buy vintage.`

### CTA Rules

Use one primary CTA per asset.

- Product selling to Vietnamese buyers: `DM để giữ hàng`
- Product availability for foreign buyers: `DM to check size and availability`
- Visit intent: `Get Directions`
- Travel planning: `Save this for your Da Nang trip`
- Education: `Save this before your next vintage hunt`
- Social proof: `Tag us when you find your piece`

Do not stack three CTAs in one post. If a post asks people to DM, do not also push comments, saves, and directions with equal weight.

## Psychology Strategy

Use psychology ethically: make real value easier to notice and act on. Do not fake scarcity, fake provenance, or fake social proof.

### Mental Models By Buyer Path

Vietnamese DM buyers:

- Present Bias: make the reward immediate with clear availability and fast DM action.
- Scarcity: use only true one-of-one or low-stock signals.
- Mental Accounting: frame lower-priced pieces as easy impulse buys and higher-priced pieces with value proof.
- Status-Quo Bias: explain why buying curated vintage is easier and safer than digging through Shopee/Facebook groups.
- Activation Energy: make the first step tiny: `DM để giữ hàng`.

Foreign travelers:

- Loss Aversion: reduce fear of wasting a trip by showing real shop visuals, hours, address, and reviews.
- Social Proof: use reviews, customer photos, and "found us" check-ins.
- Availability Heuristic: make the shop easy to picture through alley POVs and Maps photos.
- Hick's Law: give a simple visit path: `Get Directions`, not too many options.
- Peak-End Rule: make visit content end with a clear shop reveal or satisfying product close-up.

Vintage collectors:

- Authority Bias: teach with fabric, cut, era, label, and condition details.
- Reciprocity: give useful vintage knowledge before asking for a sale.
- Contrast Effect: show rejected vs selected pieces.
- Anchoring: for premium/branded pieces, show retail/new-price context only when accurate.

### Psychology By Pillar

| Pillar | Primary mental model | Execution |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Y2K Core | Availability Heuristic | Make `Y2K Da Nang` easy to remember through repeated visual/text cues. |
| Curated Vintage | Authority Bias | Teach why fabric, cut, and condition matter. |
| Discovery And Location | Loss Aversion | Prove the shop is worth the trip with wayfinding and atmosphere. |
| Curation BTS | Contrast Effect | Show rejects before the selected piece. |
| Community And Social Proof | Mimetic Desire | Show real people wanting, wearing, revisiting, and tagging Yen May. |

### Ethical Guardrails

- Scarcity must be true.
- Do not claim "sold in 24h" unless that pattern is real.
- Do not invent origin stories.
- Do not use fake customer DMs or fake review screenshots.
- Do not overuse urgency on every post; it becomes noise.
- Use education and proof more often than pressure.

## Searchable Vs Shareable Mix

Searchable content captures existing intent.

Use for:

- `Y2K Da Nang`
- `vintage shop Da Nang`
- `how to find Yen May Vintage`
- `where to buy vintage in Da Nang`
- `Y2K shop An Thuong`

Shareable content creates desire.

Use for:

- Reject Pile.
- Alley Chronicles.
- Find Your Era.
- Hidden shop reveal.
- Anti-tourist map.
- Before/after styling.

Recommended mix for Phase 1:

- 55% searchable.
- 30% shareable.
- 15% direct product selling.

Reason:

Yen May is launching new channels, so search/discovery must come before heavy selling. Product conversion still matters because Vietnamese buyers and Instagram buyers can act quickly through DM. Keep selling present, but make it ride on search and trust.

## Channel Strategy

### Facebook

Role:

- Local buyer conversion and visit validation.

Audience:

- Vietnamese buyers.
- Foreign travelers checking shop legitimacy.

Cadence:

- 4-5 posts/week.
- 2-3 Reels/week, uploaded natively.
- 1-2 product posts/week.
- 1 discovery/location post/week.
- 1 social proof or review post/week.

Content rules:

- Use `Get Directions` as page CTA.
- Product posts can still say `DM để giữ hàng`.
- Keep captions short.
- Native upload only.
- Do not share TikTok watermark links.
- Share value-first into relevant Da Nang groups when appropriate.

Copy and psychology notes:

- Use clear Vietnamese for product selling.
- Use English or bilingual clarity for discovery posts aimed at travelers.
- Keep product captions direct: item, size, price, condition, why selected, DM.
- Use social proof and local familiarity in group posts.
- Do not make every Facebook post a sales post; alternate with location proof and reviews.

Early launch rule:

- Day 1: invite friends.
- Week 1: share into groups.
- Consider 200k-300k boost for first strong post because the page has no base.

### Instagram

Role:

- Main visual catalog, DM buying channel, and foreign-customer trust channel.

Audience:

- Foreign travelers.
- Vintage collectors.
- Vietnamese buyers who shop through DM.

Language:

- English-first for foreign-facing content.
- Vietnamese support where useful.
- English and Vietnamese only.

Cadence:

- Stories daily.
- Reels 3-4/week.
- Feed carousel 2-3/week.
- Single feed post max 1/week for true hero pieces.
- Highlights updated daily after Stories.

Content rules:

- Stories are the catalog.
- Feed is for grouped drops, curation, discovery, and hero pieces.
- Product Stories should include price, size, condition, availability.
- Use alt text for material/product recognition.
- Keep Highlights clean and useful.

Copy and psychology notes:

- Stories reduce activation energy: make it easy to reply with one question.
- Feed carousels should use education and authority, not just product display.
- English-first copy should answer traveler questions fast: what, where, hours, how to DM.
- Use "Save this" on guides and education, not on every product post.
- Use Close Friends to create a real retention loop for repeat buyers.

Recommended Highlights:

- `Y2K`
- `Vintage`
- `Tops`
- `Dress`
- `Outer`
- `Bags`
- `How to Find`
- `Reviews`
- `Sold`

### TikTok

Role:

- Discovery engine and social search engine.

Audience:

- Gen Z travelers.
- Vietnamese Gen Z.
- Thrift/Y2K searchers.

Cadence:

- 3 videos/week minimum.
- First 5 videos must be strongest.

Content rules:

- Text overlay must include `Y2K Da Nang` or `vintage shop Da Nang` when relevant.
- Say keyword in voiceover when possible.
- Use hook in first 1.5 seconds.
- Use S-S-S: Stop, Stack, Satisfy.
- Track manually before 1,000 followers.

Copy and psychology notes:

- Lead with open loops: "I rejected 27 pieces before choosing this."
- Use contrast: rejected pile first, selected piece last.
- Use sensory proof: fabric sound, label close-up, rack movement.
- Avoid brand intro at the start; the brand reveal can come after curiosity is earned.
- Close with one action: save, DM, or get directions depending on the video.

First 5 video order:

1. POV alley into shop reveal.
2. Reject Pile number 1.
3. The 1 In 300 hero product.
4. Find Your Era number 1.
5. Shop morning ASMR.

### Threads

Role:

- Lightweight daily presence and community texture.

Audience:

- Existing followers.
- People who like shop diary, taste notes, and drops.

Cadence:

- 3-5 posts/week.

Content types:

- Drop notes.
- Short styling thoughts.
- "Today in the shop" notes.
- Micro curation explanations.
- Cross-post reminders for Reels/Stories.
- Quiet observations from the alley.

Rules:

- Keep it human and concise.
- No hard-sell overload.
- Use it to make the brand feel alive between bigger posts.

Copy and psychology notes:

- Threads should create mere exposure and familiarity, not carry heavy conversion.
- Use small observations that reinforce taste.
- Mention drops and hours lightly, without sounding like a catalog feed.
- Good Threads posts should feel like notes from the shop, not ads.

Example formats:

- `One piece passed today because the fabric felt right before the label mattered.`
- `New Y2K rack is up. DM on IG if you want first look.`
- `The alley is quiet this morning. Open 10:00-21:00.`

### Google Maps

Role:

- Local search and visit conversion.

Audience:

- Foreign travelers.
- Nearby visitors.
- People searching while already in Da Nang.

Cadence:

- Photo updates: 3-5/week.
- Google Posts: 2/month minimum.
- Review responses: within 48 hours.
- Q&A: set up core questions immediately.

Content rules:

- Photos should show exterior, alley path, interior, racks, product close-ups, and customers where permitted.
- Description should include `Y2K`, `curated vintage`, `Da Nang`, `An Hai Dong 1`.
- Q&A should answer how to find the shop, opening hours, payment, and whether tourists can visit.

Copy and psychology notes:

- Google Maps content should reduce regret aversion: no one wants to waste a trip.
- Photos must answer: "Is this real, open, findable, and worth visiting?"
- Q&A should remove friction before travelers arrive.
- Review prompts should ask for honest specifics: Y2K, vintage, Da Nang, hidden shop, helpful staff, good finds.

Core Q&A topics:

- How do I find the shop?
- What are the opening hours?
- Do you sell Y2K clothing?
- Can I DM before visiting?
- Do you have vintage linen/silk/denim?

## Price And Offer Framing

Use different copy logic by price tier. Do not explain every item the same way.

### 150k-250k: Impulse Tier

Buyer psychology:

- Present Bias.
- Low friction.
- Quick DM decision.

Content strategy:

- Keep copy short.
- Show fit, size, price, and availability.
- Use real urgency if the item is likely to move quickly.

Copy examples:

- `150k. One piece only. DM để giữ hàng.`
- `Easy Y2K top for Da Nang days. Size S/M. DM to check availability.`
- `Món này thường đi nhanh. Inbox nếu muốn giữ.`

### 300k-500k: Considered Tier

Buyer psychology:

- Mental Accounting.
- Value justification.
- Contrast Effect.

Content strategy:

- Explain fabric, condition, cut, and wear value.
- Compare against short-life fast fashion without attacking.
- Use "Budget vs Yen May" content here.

Copy examples:

- `500k for a piece you will keep reaching for, not wear twice and forget.`
- `Đắt hơn một chiếc áo thường. Nhưng form, vải và độ bền là lý do nó được chọn.`

### 1M+: Premium/Branded Tier

Buyer psychology:

- Anchoring.
- Regret Aversion.
- Authenticity proof.

Content strategy:

- Show label, hardware, stitching, condition, and retail/new-price context only when accurate.
- Use more proof before asking for DM.
- Make authenticity details visible in carousel/video.

Copy examples:

- `Retail reference: [x]. Yen May price: [x]. Condition: [x]. DM for details.`
- `For this tier, the details matter: label, fabric, condition, and why it is still worth wearing now.`
